I. The Central Problem: Over-Reliance on DSA and CP
The prevailing advice given to college students is to focus heavily on Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA) and competitive programming (CP) in their final year to secure a job [1]. This cycle remains common, even 15 years later, leading to a general lack of real-time application understanding among students [1, 2].
Many students, particularly those in India, enter engineering often due to peer pressure rather than genuine passion [2]. This leads them to engage in the same activities as everyone else, automatically skyrocketing competition [3].
Technology is vast, encompassing domains like Web 3, AI, and XR, and programming (including DSA, CPP, and OOPs) is only a fraction of this landscape [2].
II. Fah's Alternative Path: Early Exploration and Strategy
Fah, a GitHub Campus Expert and CNCF Ambassador working as a software developer for a top US-based startup, represents the success achievable through an alternative approach [4, 5]. Her strategy focused on contributing to open source, building networking, talking to the right minds, and gaining practical understanding of applications [4].
A. Initial Realizations and Exploration
• Early Start: Fah started exploring alternatives in her first year of college [6]. Although she performed DSA "religiously every day" on platforms like LeetCode, she prioritized exploring almost every other technological field [6, 7].
• Motivation: The primary drive was the desire to "get out of the rat race" [3] and avoid the increased competition resulting from everyone pursuing the same path [3]. She sought to explore technology at the infrastructural and ground levels [8].
• Proactive Search: Her exploration included sending hundreds of DMs to startups and companies offering to work for free [7].
• Diverse Experience: She gained experience through various paths, including an internship in XR/mixed reality, working with edtech companies, and taking a course on Web 3 [7].
B. The Role of Open Source
• Catalyst Program: Fah's involvement began when she joined the GitHub Campus Expert program by the beginning or middle of her second year [6]. This role required advocating for open source, which served as the "mindblowing" spark for her contributions [7].
• Building in Public: She started her GitHub account and began exploring projects, leading her entire journey to "flip" [7].
• Real-Time Contribution: In her second year, her interest in open source led her to join Kubernetes, one of the world's largest open source projects [8]. This demonstrates how students who master DSA should instead contribute to real-time projects where they can interact with people globally [8].

Fah stresses that awareness about opportunities is often lacking among students, and it "does not comes walking to you"; it must be actively pursued [8, 9].

B. The Value of Active Awareness and Networking
• Action is Key: Awareness is gained through extensive exploration, searching via Google, asking questions, and critically, taking action after receiving answers [9].
• Networking over Perks: While perks like a fully sponsored trip to GitHub Universe are nice, the fundamental value of these events is the networking they afford [9, 10]. This includes interacting with figures like the co-founder of GitHub or top minds in San Francisco [10].
• Strategic Conference Attendance: Conferences should be viewed as opportunities to build networks, not just to collect "swags" or relax [15, 16]. Fah confirms that attending conferences and networking proactively led directly to multiple subsequent opportunities, including two to three internships [16].
IV. Career Outcomes: The Profile That Gets Hired
Fah's current role at Aquati, a US-based, Kubernetes-native startup, was secured through a standard application process, but her pre-existing profile was the compelling factor [17].
• Open Source Portfolio: Her open source portfolio was the primary reason for her hiring [17]. Contributing for a long time allowed her to build her network and profile by coding and building "in public," which helped Aquati identify her [17].
• Community Alignment: Her status as a CNCF Ambassador and participation in events like CubeCons were "very much helpful," particularly because Aquati is a Kubernetes-native company [13, 17].
Fah confirms receiving multiple offers and believes success is a mixture of skills, luck, networking, and crucially, hard work [18, 19]. While luck plays a role, her achievements have been constant over nearly four years, suggesting the outcome is systemic and requires the ability to strategize the entire process [18, 19].
V. Reflection and Final Advice
Fah views everything she has done in technology as eventually complementing the next activity [15]. Although she doesn't use DSA daily in her non-Google/Amazon role, the practice helped build patience and resilience [15].
The overarching message is to think beyond DSA:
• Think Out of the Box: Students must understand that if everyone is focusing on the same thing (like DSA, or currently, AI models/agents), they need to think out of the box and proactively think ahead of the current trend to discover something new and "stay ahead of the curve" [5, 19].
• Focus Energy: Students should dedicate at least half the effort they spend on DSA towards the applications required to get into these student programs and related opportunities [20]. Putting one's energy in the right direction is crucial [21].
• Perspective: This discussion is not "against DSA," which is beneficial for problem-solving, but aims to present the "other perspective" and encourage students to think beyond it [21].

In essence, while traditional DSA may build the internal engine of a coder, focusing solely on it is like tuning a race car engine but never driving it on different tracks. Exploring programs and open source is the act of getting the car out, racing in public, networking with top mechanics (founders/creators), and learning how the entire racing ecosystem (Web 3, XR, Cloud Native) actually operates, thereby opening multiple paths to the finish line.
